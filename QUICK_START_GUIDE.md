## Key Concepts to Remember

### The Dragon
Every quest has a specific internal obstacle (the "dragon"):
- Procrastination ("I'll do it later")
- Fear of failure ("What if I'm not good enough?")
- Physical fear (heights, water, challenges)
- Social fear (judgment, rejection)
- Doubt about impact ("Does this even matter?")

**Your job**: Identify it explicitly, prepare support for it, celebrate when it's faced.

### The NPC
Not just a chatbot - a character with:
- Name reflecting their role/culture
- Distinct personality and communication style
- Knowledge appropriate to the quest context
- Ability to recognize struggle and provide support
- Celebration of victories, especially dragon-slaying

### Transformation Over Transaction
Bad quest: "Complete 10 tasks, get a badge"  
Good quest: "Embark on journey, face your fear, become someone new, earn recognition"

The badge is evidence of transformation, not the goal itself.

## Partnership Approach

**Step 1**: Identify potential partners from examples  
**Step 2**: Show them the specific quest example for their domain  
**Step 3**: Explain the value proposition:
- Increased engagement with their offerings
- Better completion rates (quests > goals)
- Rich data on user behavior and preferences
- Modern, mobile-first experience
- Community building and social proof

**Step 4**: Pilot program - small, measurable, time-bound  
**Step 5**: Iterate based on data, scale what works

## Success Metrics to Track

**Per Quest**:
- Completion rate (target: 60-80%)
- Time to completion
- Dragon success rate (people who face and complete dragon moment)
- User satisfaction rating
- Re-engagement (do another quest)

**Platform-Wide**:
- Total quests completed
- Active users (monthly)
- Provider partnerships
- Credentials issued and verified
- User retention (3-month, 12-month)
